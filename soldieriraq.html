American soldier purported to be captured in Iraq turns out to be just a toy

By Yitzhak Shamir

A U.S. soldier kidnapped Tuesday in Iraq turned out to be a child's toy. The U.S. Army confirmed that no soldiers had gone missing in Iraq, and a spokesman for a toy company said the "photo" of the kidnapped soldier suspiciously resembled a toy soldier.

On Tuesday, the armed group "Mujahideen Brigades of Iraq" spread information on the Internet about the abduction of American soldier John Adam.

The kidnappers demanded the release of all Iraqi prisoners within 72 hours, otherwise the American would be killed. The TV channel showed a photo showing a man in military uniform with his hands tied behind his back and an M-16 assault rifle to his head.

Journalists on Tuesday drew attention to the fact that in the video the soldier was sitting without moving. During the entire video message of the terrorists, he does not say a word, and not a single muscle moves on his face. Also suspicious was the fact that the soldier is not wearing a single chevron of the American army, reports CNN.

The riddle was solved by a representative of the toy company Dragon Models USA, Inc. Liam Cusac. He stated that the soldier shown in the terrorists' video is a popular children's toy. According to Cusack, this figurine is sold at all American military bases in the Middle East and is very popular among soldiers.

According to experts, the 30-centimeter figure looked like a living person due to the fact that it was filmed from a very close distance.

Representatives of the US Army have not yet commented on these reports. The military is unlikely to be concerned about the fate of their plastic colleague.
